    About

    The following project is based around data traces. Whatever we do, wherever we are, by living in a media-saturated social world we leave behind footprints of our media use that compile an archive of “digital traces”. This can be done when we upload photographs or write comments on the timelines of digital platforms, we leave an enduring imprint of our presence there. Vice versa, sometimes we are unaware of the process as an unintended side effect of our media related practices. For example, when we are using a search engine, reading newspapers online or posting on Facebook. Digital traces have evoked a sophisticated and controversial methodological discussion that represents a new challenge to media and communications. The aim of the following project is to make individuals more aware about the online world. As although we often hear the term digital trace, many people have very little understanding of the plurality of the digital traces that we leave behind, or their social implications. I aim to demonstrate and help those develop an understanding that data is tracked every time you visit a webpage or an application .

    Definitions

    Physical Trace

    The investigation of physical trace evidence involves examining the remnants of the subject’s past behavior. Physical traces can be subject can be unintentional and invisible for example, fingerprints. Both unintentional and invisible physical traces refer back to us in some way and reveal details about us.

    Digital Trace

    Digital trace refers to your unique set of traceable digital activities, actions, contributions and communications manifested on the internet or on digital devices. It constitutes a cultural artefact in a virtual settlement. It is come form of evidence of the activity of a user. Digital traces may be records stored in databases or log files which can be accessed by researches through the use of APIS or WebCrawler’s. Many people leave hundreds of digital traces every day. Digital traces can be intentional and visible. These include things like emails. Text, blog blogs, twitter posts, photographs, likes and posts on Facebook and Instagram. On the other hand, digital traces can also be unintentional and invisible for example, records of website visits and searches or tracking our movements and phone calls. This can make the digital world extremely dangerous. Through our everyday interaction we constantly produce digital traces, this is not only If we are online but also if we work offline on a word document.

    How can data be collected?

    Internet activity can be tracked by HTTP cookies. HTTP cookies are defined as small bits of text that are downloaded and stored by your web browser. They were designed to be a reliable mechanism for websites to remember stateful information such as, items added to someone’s online shopping cart or to record the users browsing activity such as, logging in. Cookies are used to remember pieces of information that the user previously entered into form fields such as names, passwords and payment card numbers. They preform essential functions in the modern web.

    Web beacons also track internet activity. Web beacons are a file used to keep track of your navigation through a single website or a series of websites. The web beacon operates as a tag that records an end ser’s visit to a particular web page or viewing of a particular email. They provide an ability to produce specific profiles of user behaviour in combination with web server logs. Common usage scenarios for web beacons include online ad impression counting, file download monitoring, and ad campaign performance management.
